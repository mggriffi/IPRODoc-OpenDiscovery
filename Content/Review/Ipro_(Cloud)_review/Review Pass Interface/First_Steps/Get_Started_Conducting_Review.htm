Get Started - Conduct Review

The Review module of allows users to review, analyze, and annotate case documents with efficient, easy-to-use tools. Based on the permissions set by the case administrator, users can:

  • Use advanced and/or visual search to efficiently find case data

  • View document relationships (family, duplicates, document history)

  • Code and Tag documents, add redactions and highlights, and edit document text and/or data field content (see Conduct Review)

  • View a variety of image, native, and text files concurrently from a single interface

For more information on conducting a review, see Review: Conduct Your Review.

For more information on case setup and other tasks typically done by a Case Administrator, see Review: Manage Your Review.

Before you Begin

Before working in , the product installation must be completed and your system or case administrator must define you as an user. In addition:

  • Your administrator will assign you to a group that is assigned to one or more cases and/or review batches.

  • The abilities you will have are based on the permissions defined for your group in conjunction with the case you access. For example, specific permissions exist for the following:

    • Access to the list of review passes in the Review home page.

    • Access details on the tabs in the Review pane (image, extracted text, and so on.)

  • Tagging, redacting, and other review actions

    • If you have any questions about your permissions, contact your administrator.

  • Your administrator must provide you with the URL at your company, as well as a user name and password.

Tip: Both name and password are case sensitive—make sure you obtain the correct capitalization. User names and passwords are assigned by the system administrator(s). Keep your credentials in a safe place. If you forget your login credentials, contact your administrator.

Administrative Access

Users belonging to the SuperAdmin group can open all cases in . They can also open (and will be assigned) unassigned batches in any case, or open other users’ batches.

Users who are not in the SuperAdmin group but have been given case management permissions can open all cases to which they have been assigned. In addition, they can open (and will be assigned) unassigned batches in their cases, or open other user’s batches.

Working Sessions

Once you start , your working session will remain active as long as you work in the application.

If there is a pause in your activity, the session may time out; a message will alert you that the session is idle. If this happens, click OK in response to the message and log in again.
