Common Template Questions

Q

Do I have to use a template?

A

No, you can assign permissions directly to a group in conjunction with its case assignment, or copy an existing group’s permissions.

Q

Why should I use a template?

A

Templates allow you to quickly assign commonly needed permissions to different groups and cases. You can easily modify the permissions to meet the needs of a specific case.

Q

Can I change or delete a template after I define it?

A

Templates can be modified and deleted. (Template changes will not impact groups whose permissions were based on the template.)

Q

How do I create a template?

A

See the procedures following this Q&A.

Q

How do I use a template?

A

See Assigning Permissions.

Q

Why aren’t all templates listed for cases in the Case Administration > Permission Templates section of Eclipse?

A

These templates are managing client-based. If you are in Client A’s cases, you will not see templates created for Client B.

By contrast, all templates for all managing clients are listed in System Administration > Permissions Templates.

 

Create a System Template

To create a template from System Administration:

  1. Get started:

    1. Evaluate your review needs and plan the template accordingly.

    2. Log in as a user who is a member of the Super Admin group.

    3. Click the Eclipse button, , and select Administration.

    4. If the System Administration pane is collapsed, click the pane title to expand the pane.

  2. Click Permission Templates, then .

  3. Enter a Name for the template.

    Note: Duplicate names are allowed. If you have similar templates for different managing clients, consider a useful naming convention.

  4. Select the needed Managing Client. The template will be available only for cases with the same managing client.

  5. If no template exists that can serve as a starting point, add all needed permissions and skip to step 7.

  6. If a template exists that can serve as a starting point:

    1. Click Copy Permissions From.

    2. Select the template and click OK.

    3. Make changes to permissions as needed for the new template. For tips on changing permissions, see Work in the Permissions Window.

  7. When all permissions, rights, and options have been selected, click Save.

Create a Case-Level Template

You can create a template from within an open case as follows:

  1. Get started:

    1. Evaluate your review needs and plan the template accordingly.

    2. On your site’s Eclipse website, log in as an Eclipse administrator.

    3. Open the needed case.

  2. In the Case View pane, click Case Administration > Permission Templates.

  3. Click Create New Template.

  4. Enter a Name for the template. (The managing client is based on the case opened in step 1.) The template will be available only for cases with the same managing client.

  5. If no group or template exists that can serve as a starting point, add all needed permissions and skip to step 7.

  6. If a group or template exists that can serve as a starting point:

    1. Click Copy Permissions From.

    2. Select the template and click OK.

    3. Make changes to permissions as needed for the new template.

    7. For tips on changing permissions, see Working in the Permissions Window.

  7. When all permissions, rights, and options have been selected, click Save.

View, Edit, or Delete a Template

To view, modify, or delete a template:

  1. System-level template:

    1. Log in as a user who is a member of the Super Admin group.

    2. Click the Eclipse button, , and select Administration.

    3. If the System Administration pane is collapsed, click the pane title to expand the pane.

    4. Click Permission Templates.

    5. Go to step 3.

  2. Case-level template:

    1. Evaluate your review needs and plan the template accordingly.

    2. Log in as an Eclipse administrator.

    3. Open the needed case.

    4. In the Case View pane, click Case Administration > Permission Templates.

  3. Review the list in the Templates or Case Administration tab. To find a specific template in a long list, see the tips in Use Lists.

  4. To view details of a particular template, click its name in the template list; details appear on the right.

  5. To edit template details:

  6. Click the template in the list.

    Click  and make needed changes. Editing a template does not affect permissions already assigned to groups based on a template.

    Note: The managing client associated with a template can be changed only in System Administration (not from Case Administration).

  7. Click Save.

  8. To delete a template:

    1. Click the template in the list.

    2. Click .

    3. Click Yes in response to the confirmation message. Deleting a template does not affect permissions already assigned to groups based on a template.
