Assign Permissions

The tasks that users can perform are governed by their group assignment(s) and the permissions assigned to the group in conjunction with a particular case. Several methods exist for assigning permissions to groups:

  • Assign permissions directly to a group for a specific case

  • Copy one group’s permissions to another group.

  • Use system-level templates containing basic permissions appropriate for many users.

  • Use case-level templates with permissions applicable to cases.

If you have a large number of groups that will need the same permissions, templates allow you to quickly assign the permissions to groups.

Assign Permissions to a Group

To assign permissions to a group for a specific case:

  1. Evaluate your review needs and identify the permissions that will be needed for a selected group and case. See Types of Permissions for details.

  2. Log in as an Eclipse administrator.

  3. Open the case that the group is (or will be) assigned to and for which these permissions apply.

  4. In the Case View pane, click Case Administration > Case Permissions.

  5. If the group has not yet been assigned to the case:

    1. Click Add/Remove Groups.

    2. Select the needed group and click OK.

  6. In the Groups list, click the needed group.

  7. Under the Permissions section, click Edit.

  8. If you want to base the permissions on another group or template:

    1. Click Copy Permissions From.

    2. In the Copy Permissions dialog box, select the Group or Permission Templates tab, depending on where the existing permissions are defined.

    3. Select the group or template to be copied and click OK.

  9. Select or modify permissions as needed. See Work in the Permissions Window below for details on working in this window.

  10. When all permissions, rights, and options have been selected, click Save, or click Save as Template to create a new case-level template. See Work with Templates for details on templates.

  11. Notify users of the cases they will be working on and the tasks they will be performing.

  12. If needed, repeat this procedure to define additional permissions or change permissions after they have been applied to a group for a case.
