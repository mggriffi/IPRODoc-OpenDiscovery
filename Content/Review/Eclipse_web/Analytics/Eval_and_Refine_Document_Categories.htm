Evaluate and Refine Document Categories

Categories identify specific types of documents based on a category index that is based on concepts and patterns found in case data. Your administrator may configure categories for cases containing very large amounts of data and when key issues or topics that need to be found are known. This example shows how categories appear in Eclipse.

Categories may process data more efficiently than a concept search. Contact your administrator if you believe that certain categories might benefit reviewers as they evaluate cases.

Evaluating Categories

To evaluate categories:

  1. If your administrator has provided instructions for working with categories, read those instructions. Contact your administrator if you have any questions.

  2. In the left pane, click Analytics.

  3. Expand the Categories list and locate a category that relates to your review. Documents within a particular category are identified by Eclipse Analytics based on case content and the concepts and patterns found by its algorithm-based analysis.

  4. Click the category of interest and review documents in the resulting Search Results tab.

  5. Repeat this procedure to evaluate other categories.

Adding Example Content for Categories

Content or entire documents can be identified as examples for Analytics Categories from the Extracted Text and/or Native Viewer tabs. To do so:

  1. For a selected record, open the Extracted Text or Native Viewer tab as explained in "Accessing Case Documents."

  2. Evaluate the document in the tab.

  3. To add the entire document as a category example, right-click and select Add Doc to Category.
