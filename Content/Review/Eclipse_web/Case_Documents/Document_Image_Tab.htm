Working with Document Images

About Document Images

Document images include TIFF, JPG, or PDF files created from native document files. If they exist for your case, document images appear on the Image tab in the Document Details pane of Eclipse (web).

Image Actions

In the Image tab, you can perform the tasks explained in the following table, in addition to tasks related to viewing the image, such as enlarging or rotating the image. These tasks can be performed on the individual pages of a document.

Field

Description

View images

Magnify images in a variety of ways, rotate image, and/or change image quality.

Redact text

Place a box (solid, outlined, or cross-hatched) over text on a document. Redactions also have labels to identify why the information is redacted (for example, “Attorney-Client” privilege).

Add mark-ups

Draw free-form lines on the image.

Highlight text

Emphasize text by highlighting it.

Add embedded text

Enter text comment that is visible on the image.

Add "sticky notes"

Add personal notes that are visible by double-clicking the sticky note icon, as shown on the right.

Print document images

Print documents or pages.

Getting Started with Images

To view images:

  1. Complete "Accessing Case Documents" to start working in Eclipse and install the Eclipse Viewer control.

  2. In the Document Details pane, click the Image tab.

  3. If images have not yet been viewed, click the View button. Images will be downloaded and synchronized with case records.

    4. When a record is selected, the image file is downloaded and displayed on the Image tab. A progress meter in the status bar indicates download details, similar to the following example. The download rate depends on system resources.

Change Image View

Change image quality

The image quality of some types of files can be adjusted based on the number of pixels used per character. To adjust the quality:

  1. In the Document Details pane’s Image tab, click the Pointer, .

  2. Right-click in the image and select Scale Quality > High Quality (more pixels) or Low Quality (fewer pixels).

Rotate image

To change the orientation of documents in the Image tab, take one of the following actions. Note that the cahnges made to the orientation will only affect the currently viewed page.

  1. In the Document Details pane’s Image tab, click or to rotate document’s 90 degrees right (clockwise) or right (counterclockwise), respectively.

  2. Click the button multiple times to continue to rotate images (90 degrees per click).

Magnify image area

To enlarge a particular area of an image in the Image tab:

  1. In the Document Details pane’s Image tab, position the image so the text to be magnified is visible. (See "Move image" if needed.)

  2. On the Image tab toolbar, click , then click the area to be magnified. Do not release the mouse button.

  3. Drag the mouse as needed and read/evaluate the information

  4. When finished, release the mouse button.

  5. Move the image to another area if needed, and repeat steps to magnify other areas.

  6. When finished, click or another button to stop the magnify function.

Sticky magnification

The sticky magnifier allows you to open a window for magnified content that will stay open until you close it. To use the sticky magnifier:

  1. In the Document Details pane’s Image tab, position the image so the text to be magnified is visible. (See "Move image" if needed.)

  2. Click next to the magnification button and then click .

  3. Drag across the area to be magnified. The sticky magnifier area will be marked on the image and the sticky magnifier window will open. The following figure shows an example.

  4. If the window is too small, resize it by dragging any edge to the needed size. Also, drag the window to a convenient location.

  5. As needed, complete one or more of the following actions:

    • Image tab: position the pointer in the sticky magnifier area; the pointer changes to . Drag the defined area to other locations to see other areas of the image.

    • Image tab: drag across another area of the image to redefine the sticky magnifier area.

    • Documents tab: click another record. The same relative area of the new image will be magnified.

  1. When finished, click X on the sticky magnifier window and then click (or another button) to stop the magnify function.

Resize an image

Scaling

To easily change the image size in the Document Details pane’s Image tab, complete one of the following procedures to resize the image.

  • Marquee Zoom: In the Image tab toolbar:

    1. Click the down arrow, , next to the Magnification button.

    2. Click .

    3. Drag across the image area of interest. The image will enlarge when you release the mouse button.

    4. When finished, click or another button to stop the zoom function.

  • Scale: In the Image tab, toolbar:

    1. Click the down arrow, , next to the Magnification button.

    2. Drag the slider on the scale until the image is the desired size.

    3. When finished, click in the image or click or another button to stop the previously selected function.

Move an image

To move the image within the Image tab:

  1. If your mouse has a scroll wheel, click on the image and use the scroll wheel to move up and down. If your mouse does not have a scroll wheel, complete the following steps.

  2. On the Image tab toolbar, click .

  3. Drag in the image area until the needed area is visible.

  4. When finished, click or another button to stop the move function.

Tip: These procedures apply only if the complete width and/or height of the image is not visible. For example, if the entire width of the page is visible, you will not be able to move the image from side-to-side.
