View Search Results

Search Results Tab

After you perform a search, a Search Results tab replaces the Documents tab and lists all documents meeting your search criteria. The number of documents meeting the search criteria and currently selected document in that set are listed in the navigation bar at the bottom of the tab.

Concept or Category Search Results

For Concept or Category searches (search based on a single concept or category), a coherence score is included in the last (rightmost) column of the Search Results tab as shown in this figure. Note:

  • For a Concept search, higher scores indicate a closer match to your search criteria (the concepts you are looking for).

  • For a Category search, higher scores indicate a closer match to category samples.

Note: If you define a document relationship using the Include option, related documents in the search results will show a score of 0.00. Documents meeting the search criteria (other than by this option) will have a score.

Working in the Search Results tab

You can work with the documents associated with the records appearing in a Search Results tab as you would the Documents tab. You can make redactions or apply tags, or perform any of the procedures explained in "."

In addition, a special tagging feature exists for search results—you can apply a document tag to all records listed in a Search Results tab at once, as explained in "Working with Public Tags."

Returning to the Documents tab

For each working session, search results are maintained and easily available for view. For each search, a link is added to the Documents tab, as shown in the following figure. Note that unsaved or quick searches are listed by number and results, such as 2 - (864). Saved searches also include the search name.

Tip: If you will be working with several unsaved searches, make a note the search criteria for each search when it is run, for easy recall as you work.

 

Image Tab

For a selected record in the Search Results tab, you can view “hits” in the Image tab if your case includes word coordinates. Contact your administrator if search results are not highlighted on the Image tab and you feel they should be.

Scroll throughout the image and/or change image size to see all hits; see additional details in the following tip.

Tip: If results are on individual pages, you will need to click the document’s pages to see all results in the Image tab. Or, you can see results in the Native Viewer or Extracted Text tab, both of which do not distinguish individual pages. You should also use those tabs if no highlighting exists on the Image tab.
