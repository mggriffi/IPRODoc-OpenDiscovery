Analytics Concept Search

A concept search returns documents that contain information that is conceptually similar to your search query, that is, it looks for ideas rather than specific terms or phrases.

  1. Type in your Concept. The search phrase should include text that will adequately describe the concept(s) of interest. A sentence, paragraph, or even the contents of an entire document may be entered. Entering one or just a few words is not as likely to return the most relevant results.

  2. Click the down arrow to select the Minimum Similarity. This number can be changed as you run the search to manipulate the search results.

    • A typical value is 50.

    • A higher number represents higher precision but less recall.

    • A lower number represents lower precision but higher recall.

  3. Click the down arrow to select your Analytic Index.

  4. When finished, click ,or .

    Note: An analytic index must be configured before you can run a concept search.
